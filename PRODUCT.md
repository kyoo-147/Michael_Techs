# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Primary users are recruiters, technical collaborators, founders, and potential clients who want to understand Michael quickly. They arrive in a scanning mindset first, then switch into a deeper reading mode if the work feels credible.

Secondary users are readers who discover essays or notes through search and want a clean long-form reading surface with clear paths back into projects, background, and contact.

## Product Purpose
This site presents Michael as a calm builder who ships practical AI systems, writes clearly, and thinks in products. Success means a first-time visitor can understand who Minh is, what he builds, how he thinks, and how to contact him within one short session.

The site must support two durable content loops: publishing essays in Markdown and publishing project case studies in Markdown or MDX. It also needs to be easy to deploy statically and easy to maintain through GitHub.

## Brand Personality
Calm, exacting, grounded.

The tone should feel practical, thoughtful, and trustworthy rather than loud, trendy, or over-styled. Visitors should come away with the sense that Minh is serious about craft, clear in communication, and capable of carrying real work from concept to shipped result.

## Anti-references
Do not make this feel like a generic AI startup landing page with glowing gradients, dashboard fragments, and hype copy.

Do not make it look like a noisy portfolio template with card overload, oversized decorative effects, or too many visual motifs competing at once.

Do not drift into overly academic plainness either. The site should remain minimal, but it still needs one strong visual gesture at the top and a clear editorial hierarchy.

## Design Principles
1. Lead with proof and thought together. Work and writing should feel equally intentional on the homepage.
2. Use one visual anchor, then get out of the way. The hero artwork can be memorable, but the rest of the page must stay disciplined and easy to scan.
3. Build for durable publishing. Content architecture should favor Markdown-first authoring, stable URLs, and static deployment.
4. Respect international readers from the start. English, Vietnamese, and Simplified Chinese should be first-class paths, not afterthought labels.
5. Calm is a design constraint. Hierarchy should be strong, but surfaces should remain restrained, readable, and useful.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard navigation across the full site.

Support reduced motion by keeping motion subtle and non-essential. Typography, spacing, and layout must remain readable across English, Vietnamese, and Simplified Chinese without clipping, overflow, or language-specific breakage.
